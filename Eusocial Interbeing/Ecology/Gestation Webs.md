# Gestation Webs

Gestation webs are reproductive contract systems where genomes are assembled,
selected, transported, edited, and carried across multiple species.

On Zyphos, sexual reproduction as humans know it is not the default template.
Many organisms contribute gametes, compatibility signals, symbiont lineages, or
developmental constraints into a local web. A gestator species then assembles
viable young under remembered ecological terms.

## Mother-Tree Webs

[[Mother Trees]] are one major gestator pattern. A mother tree does not merely
receive pollen from nearby trees. It receives genetic offerings from many
species in its local contract web, compares them against local memory, and
decides which combinations are worth gestating.

This does not mean every species can freely become every other species.
Development is bounded by compatibility, immune policy, tissue architecture,
symbiont inheritance, and the tree's own political interests. Gene exchange is
web-wide, but bodies remain negotiated lineages rather than soup with ambitions.

Different regions can therefore support different eusocial contract webs. A
forest may raise Airawa, defensive symbionts, climbing partners, pollinator
lineages, archive insects, and companion grazers under one gestation regime. A
neighboring wetland may use related trees, different pollinators, and different
rules of merit.

## Pollinator Function

Pollinator-equivalents are gamete transport organs for the whole local web.
They do not only move plant pollen. They collect gametes, tissue traces, immune
signatures, performance residues, and courtship analogues from organisms that
the gestation web recognizes as reproductive contributors.

Because they are selecting biological futures, serious pollinators must have
more than appetite. They need memory, route knowledge, sensory discrimination,
social trust, and enough network connection to judge whether an individual is
performing well under local terms. Strength, health, restraint, craft, treaty
keeping, courage, fertility, ecological service, and symbiont stability can all
be reproductive signals.

This makes pollination political. A pollinator can reward a useful individual,
ignore a dangerous one, preserve a marginal lineage, carry contraband heredity,
or become an imperial surveillance organ.

## Morphological Invariants

- Mobile web species need protected gamete-secretion organs. Exposed decorative
  reproductive interfaces are bad engineering in a world where predation,
  war, climbing, grooming, and political attack are routine.
- For Airawa-like climbers, the reproductive interface belongs near an armored,
  retractable neural-whip organ: close enough to exchange memory-bearing
  signals, protected enough not to make personhood or fertility hostage to a
  single exposed braid.
- Gamete secretion, neural connection, and social signaling may share tissue,
  but they do not have to be the same act. A person can connect to a network
  without automatically consenting to reproductive collection.
- Pollinator attraction must be readable by pollinators, not merely by mates.
  Performance leaves chemical, electrical, symbiotic, behavioral, or crafted
  signals that transport species can evaluate.
- Gestators own assembly and development. Pollinators own routing and initial
  selection pressure. Mobile contributors own consent, display, refusal, and
  the risks of being judged by the route.
- Reproductive access is ecological infrastructure. Controlling gestators,
  pollinator routes, compatibility records, or secretion rites is population
  policy, not private romance.

## Design Consequences

Airawa morphology should not imply ordinary mammalian sex with exotic costumes.
Their bodies should read as participants in a public reproductive ecology:
armored connection organs, visible health and contract signals, grooming rites,
pollinator-safe secretion windows, and fashion that makes lineage obligations
legible.

Imperial heredity policy is therefore control over a pipeline: contributor
assessment, gamete collection, pollinator routing, tree assembly, symbiont
inheritance, developmental conditions, and legal recognition after birth. Any
story that treats eugenics as mere mate choice has already lost the machinery.
