# Nation Scale Interbeings

Nation scale interbeings are the largest ordinary political organisms on Zyphos.
They are not metaphors for nations. They are multispecies, memory-bearing,
territorial systems whose collective behavior can take on nation-like agency.

At this scale, sovereignty is boundary management. A nation scale interbeing
decides what may cross its tissues, routes, archives, reproductive commons, and
immune fronts. Boundary permeability, in all its local variations, is foreign
policy.

## Working Taxonomic Placeholders

These names are provisional Latin-heavy phylogenetic handles until Weksa can
render culture-specific names from local languages. They follow familiar
taxonomic conventions as a design scaffold, not Earth ancestry.

- Domain: `Memoriobionta`, memory-endosymbiotic life.
- Superclade: `Contractilia`, organisms whose survival depends on explicit
  memory-bearing ecological contracts.
- Clade: `Polybionta`, multispecies bodies with stable internal role
  differentiation.
- Class: `Regnobionta`, nation scale interbeings with territorial agency.
- Order: `Permeabiliformes`, naturally grown interbeings with graded borders.
- Order: `Clausiformes`, interbeings organized around sealed or nearly sealed
  boundaries.
- Family: `Archivoridae`, mother-tree-centered archival polities.
- Family: `Viatoridae`, migratory route polities.
- Family: `Mycoviariidae`, fungal-road polities.
- Family: `Chorallidae`, reef and waterway polities.
- Family: `Imperiidae`, engineered imperial superorganisms.

Example binomials and trinomials should stay italicized and provisional: *Silva mater
archivora*, *Pratum pactivorum migrans*, *Mycelioviae candelariae*, *Chorus
liminalis*, *Imperium airavense clausum*.

## Boundary Permeability

A border on Zyphos is not a line. It is a living argument about what kinds of
traffic the interbeing can survive.

Permeability varies by channel:

- nutrient traffic;
- reproductive access;
- route permission;
- predator and scavenger passage;
- guest symbionts;
- pathogens and immune analogues;
- memory exchange;
- archival copying;
- taboo, ritual, and legal payloads;
- military organisms and engineered memetic packets.

Most native nation scale interbeings do not hold one uniform policy. A marsh
front may welcome fungal couriers while blocking grazer herds. A cliff nesting
system may accept Sa'auei'a midwives but reject Airawa archive pilgrims. A
mother-tree polity may share weather memory and refuse ancestry memory in the
same negotiation.

This makes diplomacy granular, slow, and embodied. A treaty can be a pollinator
route, a temporary immune amnesty, a breeding-ground guest law, a fungal toll, or
a remembered injury that closes a valley for three generations.

## Native Forms

### *Silva mater archivora*

Mother-tree archival nations are dense, rooted, and memory-rich. They preserve
ancestry, ecological precedent, repair protocols, and legal memory through tree,
mycorrhizal, insect, bird-analogue, and Airawa partner systems.

Their borders are often porous to food and rumor but strict around archival
copying. Foreign bodies may enter, trade, mate, and heal there without ever being
allowed near the deep memory organs.

### *Pratum pactivorum migrans*

Migratory pasture nations are route bodies. Herds, soil films, seasonal bloom
corridors, scavenger guilds, mineral licks, and mobile families form a moving
political metabolism.

Their territory changes shape across the year. A map that freezes them in place
is already lying. Their borders are invitations, closures, and debt registers
that travel with bodies instead of fences.

### *Mycelioviae candelariae*

Fungal-road nations are logistics organs that have become sovereign. They bind
groves, breeding grounds, shelters, wetlands, and corpse gardens through dim
routes of nutrition and memory.

They are hospitable by default and unforgiving by accounting. A traveler who
feeds the road may receive passage, warning, and shelter. A traveler who cheats
the road may find every safe path politely unavailable.

### *Chorus liminalis*

Choir reef nations occupy coasts, river mouths, drowned forests, and eclipse-dark
shallows. Their borders follow pressure, current, mineral taste, larval drift,
and seasonal migration rather than dry land.

They negotiate through harbor access, spawning shelter, storm prediction,
predator concealment, and memory carried by swimmers and filter colonies.

## The Imperial Exception

*Imperium airavense clausum* is a nation scale interbeing, but it is not a
natural member of the patchwork. It is an artificial `Clausiformes` system built
by the [[Airawa Empire]] through the [[Biological Memetic Engine]].

Its boundaries are deliberately impermeable to uncontrolled memory traffic. It
allows only the exact amount of autonomy, local variation, and cellular
creativity needed to keep its agents maximally productive. The structure is not
deadly because every part is crushed flat. It is deadly because every part is
permitted to move precisely where motion serves the whole.

From the perspective of tribal Airawa and native interbeings, the empire is
alien in the most practical sense: a living nation whose internal topology no
longer resembles negotiated ecology. It has rituals, lineages, memories, and
species partners, but they have been re-authored into a single artificial utility
system that venerates its founding tribe's blood and memes while becoming
something that tribe could not have understood.

## Design Use

When adding a large ecology or polity, define:

- which channels its borders admit, meter, or reject;
- what scale owns memory authority;
- how reproductive access is granted;
- what local autonomy is allowed;
- how it punishes betrayal;
- what outsiders mistake for politics when it is actually immune response;
- what outsiders mistake for nature when it is actually law.
