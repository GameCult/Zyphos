# Sa'auei'a Family Units

Sa'auei'a family units are squad-sized mobile groups. They combine kinship, defense, childcare, craft, trade, ritual practice, ecological negotiation, and memory keeping.

## Scale

The unit should be small enough for personal trust and large enough for specialized roles. Wolf-pack comparison is useful for movement, hunting, and kinship pressure, but the units also function as schools, embassies, workshops, caravans, and oath groups.

## Social Logic

Families earn status by maintaining good remembered relations across many ecological partners. A family that hoards, wounds, or breaks route obligations may still physically travel, but the continent stops opening itself to them.
